# Competitor and substitution matrix

Snapshot date: 2026-07-18

## Direct search result

No exact product was found that locally reconciles a repository’s Google OAuth runtime scopes with secret-free consent/public configuration and emits a scope-by-scope submission evidence pack. Absence from a search is not proof that no competitor exists.

## Adjacent products and substitutes

| Alternative | What it does well | Why a buyer may choose it | ScopeParity differentiation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Google Cloud Console and official checklists | Authoritative scope classification and submission UI | Free, first-party, always required | Does not inventory distributed source locations or maintain a repo-side evidence contract |
| CloudSponge Setup Concierge | Human setup and demo help for CloudSponge Contact Picker customers | Done-for-you and vendor-specific expertise | ScopeParity is self-serve, cross-stack, credential-free, and makes no approval guarantee |
| OAuthLint | Broad static analysis for OAuth/OIDC/JWT/session security anti-patterns | Open source, many languages, mature CI/report formats | ScopeParity does not scan auth security; it reconciles Google review surfaces and recording evidence |
| Manual spreadsheet/checklist | Flexible and free | No new tool or dependency | Cannot prove what tracked source actually requests or catch drift on a pull request |
| General consultants | Can reason about policy, use case, and reviewer correspondence | Human judgment and accountability | Outside ScopeParity’s model; not scalable for a solo self-serve business |

## Competitive guardrails

- Do not compete with OAuthLint on implementation security.
- Do not compete with Google on scope classification or submission workflow.
- Do not recreate a consultancy behind a self-serve landing page.
- Do not broaden to restricted-scope assessment.
- Win on the auditable join between source, secret-free config, public identity, and video evidence.

## Replacement risk

Google could add an official preflight or API that exposes all consent-screen values. If it does, ScopeParity must either become a thin open-source adapter or stop. The defensible asset is the versioned evidence contract and fixture accuracy—not private access to public requirements.
